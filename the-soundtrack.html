The Soundtrack

What we listen to when we cook

It all starts with a sound...

A la Quentin Tarantino when conjuring ideas for films, we use music as an inspiration for our food. And we listen to anything. From classic American rock inspiring us to make that cornerstone of North American cuisine, to far eastern pop motivating us to make a Korean infused sandwich. No kitchen or cooking activity is complete without accompanying music. So listen up, let the music take you to faraway places, keep those smell receptors open, and with a pinch of imagination you will be able to taste the ingredients as you listen.

Journey - Don't Stop Believin' // This is a tasty burger

Like countless others, we first heard this song during the final episode of The Sopranos. The series which had us saying moozadell every 5 minutes also inspired us to make a classic American dish. The image of Tony sitting in the diner, putting this song on the jukebox, and chowing down, what could be more American than that? Now, everyone, “Just a small town girl, livin' in a lonely world…!”

The Cambodian Space Project - Pros Kangaroo // Bulgogi Beef Sandwich

This fantastic Cambodian band resurrecting a country’s musical heritage that was almost eradicated is an inspirational story and is well worth checking out further. It inspired us to try and make a far eastern inspired dish with plenty of zest and spice. Although not strictly Cambodian, the Korean red pepper paste sandwich is what we came up with. Imagine tucking into this sandwich in a South East Asian bar, with a beer, and this song on in the background, heaven.
