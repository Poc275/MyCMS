The Inspiration

Where cooking meets pop culture

Brief intro here as to who we are and what we're hoping to achieve. Introduce ourselves with photos.

Peter

I was, probably like most other people, transfixed by celebrity chef culture. I'd spend hours watching these modern day Prometheans wave a sharp knife and pan in awe whilst thinking that I couldn't possibly do that. This is simply not true, if you try you realise that the only reason cookery is held in such high regard is that you haven't really tried doing it. It is a self-fulfilling prophecy of procrastination so just find something nice that you’d like to eat and start making it now, right now, do it now, NOW!

The Music

It all starts with a sound. A la Quentin Tarantino when conjuring ideas for films, we use music as an inspiration for our food. And we listen to anything. From classic American rock inspiring us to make that cornerstone of North American cuisine, to far eastern pop motivating us to make a Korean infused sandwich. No kitchen or cooking activity is complete without accompanying music. So listen up, let the music take you to faraway places, keep those smell receptors open, and with a pinch of imagination you will be able to taste the ingredients as you listen.

Journey - Don't Stop Believin' // This is a tasty burger

Like countless others, we first heard this song during the final episode of The Sopranos. The series which had us saying moozadell every 5 minutes also inspired us to make a classic American dish. The image of Tony sitting in the diner, putting this song on the jukebox, and chowing down, what could be more American than that? Now, everyone, “Just a small town girl, livin' in a lonely worrrrld…!”

The Books

What do we read between cooking? You'd be surprised to hear that it isn't a lot of cookery books!

A brief history of time - Stephen Hawking

We are very interested in science here on Blog Standard Food, to be honest we don't understand how you could not be either. Learning about nature is to enter a magical world of wonder and amazement, which once started you can never give up. The universe is a keen area we are interested in, you may have noticed a scattering of quotes on this subject amongst the articles, so what better way to start than to have one of the greatest minds ever explain the mystery to us. It is a beautifully written book, we won't lie, some of the subject matter can be tough to understand, but it is more accessible than you think. For books in a similar vein try Cosmos by Carl Sagan (the great explainer), anything by Brian Cox or NDGT, or for something a little more entertaining try A Short History of Nearly Everything by Bill Bryson.
